# The very first letter of the founder - the Manifesto

I believe humanity is facing a crisis far deeper than politics, economics, climate, or a new technology.

We were promised connection.
We got loneliness.

We were promised pure information.
We got deception.

We were promised freedom, but our time was bought, sold, manipulated, and turned into someone else's business model.

We were promised progress.
We got a bunch of apps to make you work harder and still feeling like you're falling behind a race nobody agreed to enter.

We were promised the bright future.
We got anxiety, FOMO, and constant stress.

We were promised communities.
We got the society where you are drifting in a world full of people, yet completely invisible.

We were promised new better world.
We got millions of people who no longer know who they are, what they stand for, or why they wake up in the morning.

We were told to consume more, react more, watch more, scroll more.
But human beings were not born to consume.
We were born to create.
To grow.
To contribute.
To thrive and be happy.
To matter.

The greatest lie of our time is that happiness comes from having more.
The truth is that happiness comes from becoming more, give more, be yourself, follow your passion, and make positive impact on others.
The world does not suffer because there are too few successful people.
The world suffers because millions of people have never discovered their purpose, never brought meaning in their lives, and never found a way to turn it into something that improves life for others.
And now, as new technologies transforms the world, this question becomes even more urgent.
If computers and robots can do more and more of what we do, then what remains uniquely human?
Meaning. Purpose. Compassion. Sentience. Contribution.

That is why I am building this.
Not another social network.
Not another productivity tool.
Not another platform designed to capture your time.
I refuse to build technology that profits from addiction, outrage, division, comparison, insecurity, or manipulation.
I refuse to measure success by time you spent but by lives that changed. If in a couple of years you need us less because you found your people and your purpose, we did our job.
I believe your data belongs to you.
I believe transparency is not optional.
I believe open source creates trust.
I believe you should decide, not manipulative algorithms.
I believe purpose beats profit, every single time they disagree. It is not just a phrase, it is our moral compass behind every hard call we make.
I believe people should be treated as creators, not products.
I believe borders, languages, governments, generations, incomes that divide us are weaker than the values that unite us.
I believe a person in Egypt, Brazil, France, India, Japan, or USA can work together toward the same meaningful goal and become part of the same human story.

My vision is simple.
A world where every person can discover who they truly are.
A world where every person can find a purpose, reach it, be happy and leave a lasting impact.
A world where strangers become friends moving towards the same direction.
A world where communities are built around contribution and meritorious change rather than consumption.
A world where millions of people unite around creating real positive impact instead of fighting endless wars.
A world where meaning becomes more valuable than devastating success.
A world where humanity remembers that we share one planet, one future, and ultimately one destiny.
If you have ever felt lost.
If you have ever wondered whether your life could mean more.
If you have ever wanted to create something larger than yourself.
Then this is for you.
I'm not asking you to do anything yet. I don't want to capture your time, I want to liberate it. 
I'm not selling a product. I intend to return your life — the real you.
I'm asking you to remember one thing: you were never meant to be lost and invisible. 
You were meant to matter.

Welcome home.

— The Founder
